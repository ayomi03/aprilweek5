Komodo Dragon

Komodo dragon females do not always need to mate with a male to give birth. Researchers found that two of these dragon females living in captivity in Europe laid viable eggs without insemination from a male.

  • KINGDOM: Animalia
  • PHYLUM: Chordata
  • CLASS: Reptilia
  • ORDER: Squamata
  • SUBORDER: Sauria
  • FAMILY: Varanidae
  • GENUS: Varanus
  • SPECIES: V. komodoensis
  • BINOMIAL NAME: Varanus komodoensis (Ouwens, 1912)

Although the Komodo dragons appear massive and bulky, they can run surprisingly fast.

Komodo dragon is a vulnerable species. These animals generally tend to avoid humans and shy away from human presence. They do not attack unless provoked but some anecdotal claims of unprovoked attacks exist.
